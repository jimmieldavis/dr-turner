# Finding Your North Star: A Companion Workbook
### Based on *Uncertainty and Hope Can Coexist* by Dr. Shairi Turner
**Scope Document | Version:** v01 | **Date:** 14 May 2026

---

## BLUF

One comprehensive workbook. Not per-chapter. Anchored to four memoir chapters. Target audience: adults who have read the memoir and want to go deeper, plus clinical, corporate, and speaking engagement use cases. Requires original content: exercises, prompts, reflection questions. Not a manuscript reformat. Scope as a standalone project beginning after memoir v3 is final.

---

## Product Identity

**Working title:** *Finding Your North Star: A Companion Workbook*
**Subtitle:** *Reflections and Practices from* Uncertainty and Hope Can Coexist

**What it is:** A structured guided-reflection workbook tied to the memoir's core themes. Readers work through their own story alongside Dr. Turner's.

**What it is not:** A summary of the memoir. A per-chapter breakdown. A self-help book that stands alone without the memoir.

---

## Target Audience

**Primary:** Adults who have read the memoir, seeking a structured practice to apply its themes to their own life. Professionals in healthcare, education, social work, and caregiving are the core demographic.

**Secondary use cases:**
- Speakers: sell from stage at $25 to $30 alongside the memoir. Bundle pricing: memoir + workbook.
- Clinical training: therapists and social workers using it in supervision or group settings.
- Corporate wellness: organizations running mental health programming for staff.
- Book clubs: structured discussion and reflection tool.
- Academic courses in public health, social work, medicine.

## Structure

Six sections. The North Star metaphor is the spine throughout. Each section anchors to specific memoir chapters and builds on the previous one.

---

### Introduction | Before You Begin

**Memoir anchor:** Introduction, Chapter 1 ("To Be a Healer")
**Purpose:** Orient the reader to the workbook's frame. Establish that this is not about having answers; it is about learning to hold uncertainty alongside hope.

**Content:**
- A brief word from Dr. Turner (original, 1 page)
- How to use this workbook (practical instructions)
- One opening reflection: *When did you first feel the pull toward something bigger than yourself?*

---

### Section 1 | What Called You

**Memoir anchor:** Chapter 2 ("The Making of a Doctor"), Chapter 3 ("Breaking and Becoming")
**Theme:** Origin stories. The moments that planted the seeds. Retrospective reframing.

**Reflection questions (4):**
1. What experience first told you what you were supposed to do, even if you didn't recognize it at the time?
2. Where in your life have you broken in order to become something new?
3. What did you carry into your work from your childhood? Name it specifically.
4. What is the thing you do that doesn't feel like work to you? Where did that come from?

**Exercise:** Timeline of Seeds. Map six to eight moments in your life that now, in retrospect, led you exactly where you are. Label each one: *I didn't know it then, but...*

**North Star Action:** Write one sentence finishing this: *I was made for this because...*

---

### Section 2 | Finding Your North Star

**Memoir anchor:** Chapter 4 ("Finding My North Star")
**Theme:** Identifying the values and convictions that orient all decisions. Not goals, not titles. The North Star beneath the plan.

**Reflection questions (4):**
1. What would you still do if no one was watching and no one was paying you?
2. What injustice makes you physically uncomfortable when you look away from it?
3. Describe a moment when you acted against your own interest because something larger required it. What drove that?
4. If someone who loves you and tells you hard truths described your North Star, what would they say?

**Exercise:** The Five-Filter Test. Name a major decision you made in the last five years. Run it through five filters: Did it align with your values? Did it move toward something or away from something? Did it require sacrifice? Would you make it again? What did it cost and what did it return?

**North Star Action:** Write your North Star in one sentence. Not your mission statement. Not your job title. The thing that does not change regardless of what you are called or where you work.

---

### Section 3 | Voice of Conscience

**Memoir anchor:** Chapter 6 ("Voice of Conscience"), Chapter 7 ("Teaching the Unseen")
**Theme:** The difference between the voices that tell you to play it safe and the voice that tells you what is right. Learning which one to follow.

**Reflection questions (4):**
1. Describe a time you heard the voice that said *this is wrong* and stayed quiet anyway. What held you back?
2. Describe a time you spoke when silence would have been safer. What happened?
3. Whose voice in your life has shaped your conscience most? What did they teach you?
4. What does your conscience know right now that you have not yet acted on?

**Exercise:** The Silence Audit. List three situations in the past year where you said less than you believed. For each: what stopped you, what it cost, and what you would say now.

**North Star Action:** Identify one conversation you have been avoiding. Commit to a date to have it.

---

### Section 4 | Healing the Healer

**Memoir anchor:** Chapter 11 ("Healing the Healers")
**Theme:** Sustainability. The cost of proximity to suffering. What caregivers and advocates owe themselves.

**Reflection questions (4):**
1. What are the signs that you are running on empty? Name them specifically, not generically.
2. Who in your life is allowed to tell you when you are too close to something? Do you listen to them?
3. Where in your work do you absorb what you cannot fix? How does it come out?
4. What does rest actually look like for you? Not what you think it should look like. What actually restores you?

**Exercise:** The Depletion Map. Draw three concentric circles. In the innermost circle, write what gives you energy. In the middle circle, what is neutral. In the outer circle, what depletes you. Now audit the last 30 days: which circle has had the most of your time?

**North Star Action:** Identify one thing in the outer circle (depleting) that you will reduce or eliminate this month. Name it and the specific action.

---

### Section 5 | Your North Star in Action

**Memoir anchor:** Chapter 12 ("Your North Star"), Conclusion
**Theme:** Moving from knowing to doing. The North Star is not a destination; it is the direction you move in even when you cannot see the end.

**Reflection questions (4):**
1. What would you do tomorrow if you knew your North Star was enough to guide you, even without a plan?
2. Who in your life is watching how you handle this? Who are you modeling this for?
3. What is the one thing you keep postponing because the conditions are not perfect yet? What are you waiting for?
4. If uncertainty and hope can coexist, what becomes possible that you have been treating as impossible?

**Exercise:** The North Star Letter. Write a letter to yourself dated five years from today. You have been moving toward your North Star for five years. What has changed? What has not? What do you wish you had started sooner?

**North Star Action:** Name one thing you will start this week. Not plan to start. Start.

---

### Closing | Both at Once

**Theme:** The workbook's final word mirrors the book's central truth: you do not have to resolve the uncertainty before you begin hoping. You do not have to finish grieving before you start building.

**Content:**
- One closing reflection prompt: *What are you uncertain about right now? And what are you hoping for, in that same moment?*
- A final word from Dr. Turner (original, half page): "Handle hard well."
- The closing line of the memoir (with permission, as a closing image).
