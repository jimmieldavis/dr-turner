## Purpose

This document gives you everything needed to run AI illustration style explorations in Midjourney (primary recommendation) or Adobe Firefly. The goal is to lock a visual direction before hiring a human illustrator, so the brief you take to a professional is precise, not aspirational.

---

## Tool Recommendation

**Midjourney** (midjourney.com) is the strongest tool for this purpose. Version 6 or 6.1. Access via Discord or the web interface.

Adobe Firefly is a secondary option if Midjourney access is not available. Firefly's style fidelity is lower for this kind of nuanced, emotionally specific children's illustration work.

DALL-E 3 (via ChatGPT) is a distant third. Adequate for rough concept exploration, not for style fidelity at the level this book requires.

## Style Rankings for This Book

Ranked by fit with the book's tone (muted, warm, emotionally honest, not whimsical):

| Rank | Style Reference | Why It Fits |
|------|----------------|-------------|
| 1 | Vashti Harrison | Soft watercolor, muted palette, cultural specificity, emotional depth. Her Black female characters carry dignity without being over-rendered. Closest match to this book's feeling. |
| 2 | Ekua Holmes | Textured collage-influenced work. Luminous. The natural world spreads (garden, birds, sky) would be extraordinary in this style. Slightly more complex production path for a human illustrator to execute. |
| 3 | Kadir Nelson | Rich painterly quality. Maximum dignity. Risk: his style reads as monumental, which may be too heavy for a contemplative children's book. Worth testing on the embrace spread (Spread 8). |
| 4 | Christian Robinson | Bold, geometric, kinetic. The brother/shoes spread (Spread 5) works here. Does not fit the book's quieter emotional register for the primary spreads. |

**Recommendation before hiring:** Run Spreads 1, 8, and 12 in Vashti Harrison style and Ekua Holmes style. Those two spreads cover the book's three emotional registers (wonder, intimacy, resolution). If one style is clearly right across all three, that is the brief.

## Evaluation Criteria

When you run these, evaluate each output against four questions:

1. **Does Maya feel like a real child, not a symbol?** The illustration should feel specific and personal, not generic.
2. **Do the two palettes (blue-gray for uncertainty, gold-amber for hope) coexist without one dominating?** This is the book's visual thesis.
3. **Is the emotional register quiet?** This book is contemplative, not energetic. If the image feels like it wants to move, it's the wrong style.
4. **Can a human illustrator execute this style consistently across 13 spreads?** Ekua Holmes's collage work, for example, requires a very specific skill set. Vashti Harrison's watercolor style is more reproducible.

## Next Step After Generation

Select the 2 to 3 best outputs across both styles. Bring those to a human illustrator search on the following platforms: **Society6**, **Upwork (illustration vertical)**, **Children's Book Insider illustrator directory**, or **SCBWI (Society of Children's Book Writers and Illustrators) member directory**. The AI outputs become the brief: "This is the feeling, the palette, the register. Can you work in this direction?"

## Budget Anchor (For Planning)

Professional children's book illustration (13 to 16 spreads, full color): $8,000 to $20,000 depending on illustrator experience and rights scope. Budget $12,000 to $15,000 as a realistic midpoint for a first-time picture book with a debut author. This is the single largest production cost for this product.
